Features

Close in Mission Control

Hold the pointer over a window thumbnail in Mission Control to reveal a close button, just like GNOME's Activities overview. Click it to close the window without leaving Mission Control.

Tile Assist

After tiling a window to the left or right half, a list of other windows appears to fill the remaining space.

Hot Corner

Move the pointer to the top-left corner of any screen to trigger Mission Control, complete with a GNOME-style ripple animation.

Green Button Fills

The maximize button expands the window to fill the screen instead of entering full screen. Click again to restore.

Dark Menu Bar

Shows a black bar behind the transparent menu bar when a window fills the screen, keeping it readable against any wallpaper.

Caps Lock Indicator

A centered onscreen notification appears whenever Caps Lock is turned on or off.

Keyboard & Mouse

Press ⌥

Press the Option key to open Mission Control.

Press ⌥ Twice

Press the Option key twice to open Spotlight Apps.

⌥-1 through ⌥-9

Open apps in the Dock by number. The Finder position is configurable.

Window Tiling

Press ⌥-arrow keys to tile windows to the left or right half, maximize, or restore to previous size.

Cut and Paste Files

Control-X then Control-V in the Finder moves files instead of duplicating them.

Home/End Keys

Home and End keys move the insertion point to the start and end of the line, just like on Linux.

Menu Key

The Menu key on PC keyboards opens a shortcut menu, even in apps that don't support it natively.

Middle-Click Paste

Middle-click a text field to paste at the click position (X11-style). Middle-click a Dock icon to open a new window. Other clicks pass through for native behavior, such as opening links in a new tab in browsers.

Scroll Zoom

Control-scroll zooms in and out in browsers, with natural or traditional scroll direction support.

Built for Control

Localized

Available in 11 languages: English, Arabic, Chinese (Simplified), French, German, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese (Brazilian), Russian, and Spanish.

Fine-Grained Control

Every feature can be individually turned on or off in the Settings window, down to individual keyboard shortcuts.

Fully Offline & Open Source

No telemetry, no network requests, no data collection. Everything runs locally on your Mac. The full source is on GitHub.

Settings

Install

Install using Homebrew:

brew install logonoff/bucket/superopt

Or download the latest release directly from GitHub Releases. The app uses ad hoc signing, so you may need to Control-click the app, choose Open from the shortcut menu, and then click Open to bypass Gatekeeper.

Permissions

SuperOpt requires Accessibility and Input Monitoring permissions in System Settings > Privacy & Security. The app asks for permissions the first time you open it.

Warning

This project is AI slop and the code has not been vetted by a human. Use at your own risk!
